Not every print job fits neatly into a standard size or quantity — and that's perfectly fine. If you need a product that isn't listed in our online store, a size that falls outside our standard options, a specialty material, or a bulk quantity that requires custom pricing, a custom quote is the right place to start. Our team will review your project and come back to you with accurate, competitive pricing tailored to exactly what you need.

## How to Submit a Custom Quote Request

<Steps>
  <Step title="Gather Your Project Details">
    Before reaching out, collect as much information about your project as possible. The more detail you provide, the faster and more accurate your quote will be. See the checklist below for what to include.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Your Contact Method">
    Submit your quote request using any of the following methods:

    - **Online form:** Fill out the quote request form on the Publications Plus website — it's the fastest way to get your details to our team.
    - **Phone:** Call us directly at **(877) 701-5087** to speak with a team member.
    - **Email:** Send your project details to [support@pubplus.net](mailto:support@pubplus.net) or [production@pubplus.net](mailto:production@pubplus.net).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Include Your Artwork or Design Files">
    If you already have print-ready artwork, attach or share it with your request. If you don't have files ready yet, let us know — our design team can help. See [Design Services](/guides/design-services) for more information.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for Your Quote">
    Our team will review your request and respond with a detailed quote. We work to turn quotes around as quickly as possible; more complex or specialty projects may take a little longer to price accurately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve and Place Your Order">
    Once you receive your quote, review the details and approve it to move forward. Your job enters production after approval and any required deposits or payments are received.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to Include in Your Request

Providing complete information upfront helps us give you an accurate quote without multiple rounds of back-and-forth. Include as many of the following details as possible:

<Accordion title="Product Type">
  Describe the type of printed piece you need — for example: business cards, banners, brochures, direct mail postcards, vehicle wraps, custom labels, etc.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Size and Dimensions">
  Provide the finished size (width × height). If you need multiple sizes, list each one separately. Include any bleed requirements if known.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Quantity">
  Tell us how many pieces you need. If you'd like pricing at multiple quantities for comparison, let us know and we can often provide tiered pricing.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Paper Stock or Material">
  Specify the substrate if you have a preference — for example: 100 lb gloss text, 14 pt card stock, vinyl, canvas, corrugated board, etc. If you're unsure, describe the intended use and our team will recommend an appropriate material.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Finishing Options">
  Include any special finishing requirements such as lamination (matte or gloss), UV coating, die cutting, folding, scoring, perforation, grommets, or mounting.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Deadline">
  Let us know when you need the finished product in hand. Share both an ideal date and a hard deadline if applicable so we can assess rush options if needed.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Artwork Status">
  Tell us whether you have print-ready files, files that need adjustment, or no artwork at all. If you need design help, reference our [Design Services](/guides/design-services) page.
</Accordion>

## File Requirements

When submitting artwork with your quote request, follow these guidelines to ensure accurate pricing and smooth production:

- **Resolution:** 300 DPI minimum at final print size
- **Color mode:** CMYK (not RGB)
- **Preferred format:** Print-ready PDF

<Tip>
  Even if your files aren't fully print-ready, send them along anyway. Our pre-press team can evaluate what adjustments are needed and factor any file prep work into your quote.
</Tip>
