## How Pre-Press Works

Before any job goes to press, our dedicated pre-press department reviews every client file to catch potential issues before they become costly mistakes. Here's what happens behind the scenes:

<Steps>
  <Step title="File Submission">
    You submit your artwork or design files along with your order. PDF files are preferred, but our team can work with a variety of formats.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preflight Check">
    Our pre-press specialists run your files through industry-standard preflight software to identify resolution issues, missing fonts, incorrect color modes, or other print-blocking problems.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Technical Corrections">
    The team adds crop marks, bleeds, and any other required print marks. If your file is in RGB, we convert it to CMYK so colors reproduce accurately on press.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approval & Production">
    Once your file passes preflight and all corrections are confirmed, it moves to production. If any significant changes were made, we'll reach out to you before printing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  For the smoothest pre-press experience, export your files at **300 DPI minimum** in **CMYK color mode** as a print-ready PDF. This reduces back-and-forth and helps your job move to press faster.
</Tip>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="What file formats do you accept?">
  We prefer **print-ready PDFs**, but our team can work with common formats including AI, EPS, PSD, and INDD. If you're unsure whether your file will work, reach out before submitting and we'll advise you.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What resolution should my images be?">
  All images and artwork should be at least **300 DPI** at the final print size. Low-resolution files (72–96 DPI, typical of web images) will appear pixelated or blurry when printed.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What is CMYK and why does it matter?">
  CMYK (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Black) is the color model used by commercial printing presses. RGB is designed for screens. If your file is in RGB, colors may shift during printing — sometimes significantly. Our pre-press team converts files to CMYK, but designing in CMYK from the start gives you the most accurate color preview.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How long does the design process take?">
  Turnaround depends on the complexity of your project. Simple file prep can often be completed quickly, while original logo design or branding projects may take several business days. Share your deadline when you request a quote and we'll do our best to accommodate it.
</Accordion>
