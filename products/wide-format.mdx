## File Preparation Requirements

Prepare your artwork to these specifications before submitting your files. Files that don't meet these standards may delay production.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Resolution — 300 DPI minimum">
    Set your document resolution to at least **300 DPI** (dots per inch) at final print size. Lower resolutions — such as the 72 DPI typical of screen graphics — will appear blurry or pixelated when printed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Color mode — CMYK">
    Design and export in **CMYK** color mode. Wide-format presses use Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, and Black inks. RGB files are converted during pre-press, which can shift colors unexpectedly. Setting CMYK from the start ensures what you see is what you get.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="File format — PDF or AI preferred">
    Submit artwork as a **PDF** or **Adobe Illustrator (.ai)** file whenever possible. These formats preserve vector elements, embedded fonts, and color profiles. Raster formats (JPEG, PNG) are accepted only when resolution requirements are met.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Bleed — 0.25 inch on all sides">
    Extend background colors and images **0.25 inches beyond the trim edge** on every side. This bleed zone ensures there are no white edges after the sign is cut to its final dimensions. Keep all critical text and logos at least 0.125 inches inside the trim line.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
