## Binding Options

The right binding depends on your page count, how readers will use the piece, and how long it needs to last. Use the comparison below to choose.

### Saddle Stitch vs. Perfect Bound

| Feature | Saddle Stitch | Perfect Bound |
|---|---|---|
| **Method** | Folded sheets stapled through the spine | Pages glued to a flat, wrap-around cover |
| **Best page count** | Up to ~64 pages | 48 pages and above |
| **Spine printing** | No (rounded spine) | Yes — print your title on the spine |
| **Lies flat when open** | Yes | Partially |
| **Cost** | Lower | Slightly higher |
| **Common uses** | Programs, newsletters, thin catalogs | Product catalogs, annual reports, thick guides |
| **Shelving/display** | Not ideal | Stands upright on a shelf |

**Choose saddle stitch** when your page count is modest, cost efficiency matters, and readers will use the piece at a table or in hand.

**Choose perfect bound** when you want a book-like appearance, a printable spine, or a page count that exceeds saddle stitch's practical range.

## File Preparation Tips

<Tip>
  Supply your interior pages and cover as **separate, press-ready PDF files**. Keep all images at 300 DPI in CMYK color mode, add 0.125 in. bleed on all edges, and embed your fonts. For perfect-bound books, ask us for the exact spine width — it's calculated from your page count and paper stock — so your cover template is accurate before you finalize your design.
</Tip>
