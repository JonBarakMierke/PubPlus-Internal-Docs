## Choosing Your Print Method

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Heat-Set (Long Runs)" icon="fire-flame-curved">
    Our heat-set department is the gold standard for medium-to-long print runs. Ink is dried with high-temperature ovens as the web press rolls, producing vibrant, consistent color across coated and uncoated roll papers. If you're printing thousands of copies and need reliable color fidelity from copy one to copy ten-thousand, heat-set is your press.

    - **Best for:** runs of roughly 5,000+ copies
    - **Paper:** coated gloss, coated matte, and uncoated roll stocks
    - **Turnaround:** 5–7 business days after proof approval
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sheet-Fed (Short-to-Medium Runs)" icon="layer-group">
    Our sheet-fed department feeds individual sheets through the press, making it ideal for shorter runs and thicker paper stocks. It offers quicker turnaround and the ability to add UV coating directly to your covers.

    - **Best for:** low-to-medium run quantities
    - **Paper:** a wide range of coated and uncoated sheet stocks, including heavier cover weights
    - **Turnaround:** typically faster than heat-set; ask for specifics at quoting
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Binding Options

Every magazine or publication needs a binding method that suits its page count and intended use.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Saddle Stitch (Staple Bound)">
    Saddle stitching folds and staples your publication through the spine. It's the most economical binding for thinner books — typically up to 64 pages — and lies flat when open. Most consumer magazines and event programs use this method.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Perfect Bound (Glued Spine)">
    Perfect binding trims the spine flat and glues a wrap-around cover to the interior pages. The result is a squared spine you can print on — ideal for publications of 48 pages or more that you want to display on a shelf or mail in an envelope. Think trade magazines, annual reports, and thick event guides.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Cover UV Coating

Protect your covers and elevate the tactile experience with UV coating, applied through our sheet-fed department.

| Coating Type | Look & Feel | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Gloss UV** | High-shine, reflective finish | Newsstand magazines, vibrant photo covers |
| **Matte UV** | Flat, non-reflective finish | Sophisticated editorial or corporate publications |
| **Soft-Touch UV** | Velvety, tactile feel | Premium annual publications, collector issues |

## File Preparation

Submit print-ready PDF files with the following specifications:

- **Resolution:** 300 DPI minimum for all images and artwork
- **Color mode:** CMYK (convert all RGB and Pantone colors before export)
- **Bleed:** 0.125 in. (⅛ inch) on all sides that bleed to the edge
- **Safe zone:** keep text and critical elements at least 0.125 in. inside the trim line
- **Fonts:** embed all fonts or convert type to outlines
- **Cover vs. interior:** supply as separate PDF files

If you're unsure whether your files are press-ready, email them to our production team and we'll review them before your job goes to press.
