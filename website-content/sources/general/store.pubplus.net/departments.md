## Pre-Press Department

### This is where it all starts

Prepress is the term used in the printing and publishing industries for the processes and procedures that occur after the creation of a design layout but before the final printing. The artwork is often provided by the customer as a print-ready PDF file created in desktop publishing.

Prepress is probably the most important step in getting a print job right. With the preflight software, the Prepress department checks all of the client's electronic files and makes necessary adjustments like adding crop marks and converting the color modes to industry-specific CMYK. When done correctly, the possibility of a poorly executed job is virtually non-existent.

### Design Requirements

Due to the nature of our offset presses, we have certain design requirements. Learn more about bleed by [clicking here](https://store.pubplus.net/print-resources).

Minimum of 300 DPI

300 DPI (dots per inch) is the recommended minimum resolution for print because it ensures sharp, high-quality images. Lower resolutions, like 72 DPI (used for screens), will result in blurry or pixelated prints.

File Recommendations

If you are sending design-ready artwork, PDF files are preferred.

Design & Export in CMYK

When designing for offset printing, it's crucial to use the CMYK color model because offset printers rely on four ink colors — Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, and Key (Black) — to produce images. Unlike RGB, which is used for digital screens, CMYK ensures that colors appear as intended in print.

### More About Binding & Finishing

After your project is printed, it moves to our expert binding and finishing team, where every detail is handled with precision. We offer a full range of services from cutting and collating to specialized binding options.

Our binding and finishing services ensure your materials are not only professionally assembled but also ready for presentation, packaging, or distribution.

#### Department Capabilities

Finishing Options

- Folding
- Collating
- Cutting
- Die-cuts
- Scoring
- UV Coating
- Hole Punch

Binding Options

Saddle Stitch

Sheets are folded in half and secured with staples along the spine. Cost-effective, quick turnaround, and lays flat for easy reading. Ideal for booklets, brochures, and catalogs.

Perfect Bound

Pages are glued together at the spine with a strong adhesive, creating a clean, square edge. Professional finish with a polished look — ideal for thicker publications.

UV Coating

UV coating provides a high-gloss finish that makes colors pop while protecting against wear. Available in Gloss, Matte, and Soft-Touch finishes.
