## Frequently Asked Questions

What type of products and services do you provide?

We are a full-service shop offering a wide range of products and services. Check out our [Products](https://store.pubplus.net/store) and [Services](https://store.pubplus.net/departments) pages for a full listing.

How do I get a quote?

The quickest way is to [submit our quote request form](https://pubplus.net/quote-request/). You can also call us and speak directly with one of our customer service reps to make sure we capture everything needed for an accurate estimate.

At what resolution should I save my photos and graphics?

Resolution should be set to 300 DPI. Images pulled from the web are often only 72–96 DPI and will appear pixelated when printed. Also save photos in CMYK color mode when possible — RGB images may not print correctly.

Why do printed colors look different from my screen?

Monitors use the RGB color model, which supports a wider spectrum than the CMYK model used by printers. CMYK generally matches 85–90% of RGB colors. When an RGB color falls outside CMYK's range, the printer substitutes the closest available color.

What file formats do you accept?

PDF is the preferred format for print-ready files. We also accept AI and InDesign files. Files should be 300 DPI with proper bleed and crop marks. Unsure? Email us at [support@pubplus.net](mailto:support@pubplus.net) before ordering.

How long does production take?

Simple jobs are often completed in less than an hour. Larger or more complex jobs may take several days depending on complexity and quantity. We always provide an accurate turnaround estimate and will work with you to hit your deadline.

What forms of payment do you accept?

We accept cash, company check, and all major credit cards. We can also set up a business account — contact us for details.

What is variable data printing?

Variable data printing personalizes each printed piece to a specific recipient. It can be as simple as unique names and addresses, or as sophisticated as recipient-specific graphics, text, and data-driven charts — making every piece in a run uniquely relevant.
