## Choosing the Right Paper Stock

The paper you select affects how colors render, how the flyer feels in hand, and whether recipients can write on it. Here's a quick breakdown of each option:

| Stock | Weight | Surface | Best For |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **60# Offset** | Light | Uncoated, matte | High-volume handouts, writable surfaces |
| **70# Offset** | Medium | Uncoated, matte | General-purpose flyers, community postings |
| **80# Gloss Text** | Medium-heavy | Coated, gloss | Retail promotions, event announcements |
| **100# Gloss Text** | Heavy | Coated, high gloss | Premium marketing pieces, bold photography |

<Tip>
  For designs with **bold colors, gradients, or photography**, choose **80# or 100# Gloss Text**. The coated surface prevents ink from absorbing into the paper, which keeps colors vivid and contrast sharp.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  If recipients need to **write on the flyer** — for example, to fill in an RSVP, sign-up form, or coupon code — choose **60# or 70# Offset**. The uncoated surface accepts pen and pencil easily, while gloss stock can cause ink to smear or bead.
</Tip>
